# Chancery Guide Rules

## Court Structure

The Chancery Division sits within the Business and Property Courts:
- **Rolls Building, London** -- main centre
- **Regional centres** -- Birmingham, Bristol, Cardiff, Leeds, Liverpool, Manchester, Newcastle
- **Judges** -- Chancery Masters (procedural), High Court Judges and Deputy Judges (substantive)

## Bundles (Chapter 15)

### Format
- Single PDF, searchable text throughout
- Bookmarks for every document in the navigation pane
- Hyperlinked index (click to navigate)
- Continuous page numbering (first page to last)
- A4 portrait orientation
- Documents in chronological order within each section

### Core Bundle
For complex cases, prepare a core reading bundle:
- Maximum 20-30 pages
- Key documents the judge needs to read in advance
- Identified in skeleton argument

### Bundle Structure
1. Index (hyperlinked)
2. Claim form / application notice
3. Statements of case
4. Court orders (chronological)
5. Witness statements (with exhibits)
6. Expert reports
7. Skeleton arguments
8. Draft order
9. Authorities bundle (usually separate)

### Filing
- File per court directions (usually 3-7 days before hearing)
- Electronic filing via CE-File where available
- Agreed bundle if possible; failing that, claimant prepares

## Skeleton Arguments (Chapter 16)

### Format
- Concise, structured document
- Maximum 25 pages (unless court permission)
- Numbered paragraphs
- Proper citations (neutral citation format)

### Structure
1. **Summary** -- what the case is about (1-2 paragraphs)
2. **Chronology** -- key dates (or reference to agreed chronology)
3. **Issues** -- what the court needs to decide
4. **Law** -- propositions of law with authorities cited
5. **Application of law to facts** -- how the law applies
6. **Relief sought** -- the specific order requested

### Filing
- Filed and served per court directions
- Usually 2 working days before hearing
- Lodge with the court (CE-File or counter)

## Applications (Chapter 14)

### Ordinary Applications
- File application notice (Form N244)
- Supported by witness statement
- Draft order
- Pay court fee
- Serve on all parties at least 3 clear days before hearing

### Without Notice Applications
- Only in genuine urgency or where notice would defeat purpose
- Duty of full and frank disclosure (disclose ALL material facts including those against you)
- Return date for respondent to be heard (usually 7-14 days)
- Failure of full disclosure: order may be discharged

### Consent Orders
- File draft order signed by all parties (or their solicitors)
- Brief recital of basis for order
- Court approves without hearing (unless it raises issues)

## Case Management

### Allocation
- Chancery Master handles procedural matters and some substantive applications
- Complex or high-value matters before a Judge
- Multi-track (almost all Chancery cases)

### Directions
Standard directions for Part 8 claim:
1. Defendant's evidence: 28 days
2. Claimant's evidence in reply: 14 days
3. Bundle: claimant to prepare, lodge 7 days before hearing
4. Skeleton arguments: 2 working days before hearing
5. Time estimate for hearing
6. Costs budgets (if applicable)

## Costs in Chancery

### Trust and estate cases
- Buckton categories apply (see scco-costs skill)
- Category 1 and 2: costs from estate fund
- Category 3: normal costs rules

### Generally
- Costs follow the event (loser pays) unless trust/estate exception
- Part 36 offers apply
- Summary assessment at hearings under 1 day
- Detailed assessment otherwise

## Hearing Preparation Checklist
- [ ] Bundle filed per directions (usually 7 days before)
- [ ] Skeleton argument filed (usually 2 days before)
- [ ] Authorities bundle prepared (hard copies if judge requests)
- [ ] Draft order prepared
- [ ] Chronology prepared (agreed if possible)
- [ ] List of issues prepared
- [ ] Reading time estimated and communicated to court
- [ ] Any pre-reading directions complied with
- [ ] Court informed of any changes to time estimate
