# CPR Part 8 Procedure

## When Part 8 Applies

Part 8 is the alternative procedure used when:
- The claimant seeks the court's decision on a question unlikely to involve a substantial dispute of fact
- A rule or practice direction requires or permits its use
- Common in Chancery Division for trust, estate, and construction of will cases

### Mandatory Part 8 (by rule/PD)
- Applications under Trustee Act 1925 (e.g., executor removal under s.50 AJA 1985)
- Construction of wills and trusts
- Applications for administration orders
- Certain probate claims (Non-Contentious Probate Rules)
- Applications under Variation of Trusts Act 1958

### Discretionary Part 8
- Any claim with no substantial factual dispute
- Court can transfer to Part 7 if substantial factual dispute emerges

## Part 8 vs Part 7

| Feature | Part 7 | Part 8 |
|---------|--------|--------|
| Claim form | N1 | N208 |
| Particulars of claim | Required (may be separate) | Not used |
| Defence | Required | Acknowledgment of service |
| Witness evidence | Exchanged per directions | Filed with claim |
| Disclosure | Standard or menu | Rarely ordered |
| Trial | Full trial with cross-examination | Hearing on papers + submissions |
| Default judgment | Available | Not available |
| Factual dispute | Expected | Should be minimal |

## Required Documents (Claimant)

### At issue
1. **Part 8 claim form (N208)** -- must state:
   - The question the claimant wants the court to decide
   - The remedy sought
   - The legal basis for the claim
   - That Part 8 applies (and why)

2. **Witness statement(s)** -- filed with the claim form, containing all evidence the claimant relies on

3. **Draft order** -- the specific order you ask the court to make

4. **Court fee** -- varies by type of application

### Additional documents (Chancery Division)
- Skeleton argument (if hearing listed)
- Bundle (per Chancery Guide Chapter 15)
- List of issues
- Chronology

## Defendant's Response

### Acknowledgment of service
- File within 14 days of service (CPR 8.3)
- State whether defendant contests the claim
- State whether defendant seeks different remedy
- File with any witness evidence in response

### No defence required
Part 8 does not use particulars of claim or a defence. The evidence is in witness statements.

## Directions

The court will typically direct:
1. Time for any further evidence
2. Whether oral evidence is needed (rare in Part 8)
3. Hearing date
4. Bundle preparation
5. Skeleton argument deadlines

## Transfer to Part 7

If a substantial factual dispute emerges:
- Court may order the claim to continue under Part 7
- Statements of case will be required
- Standard Part 7 directions apply
- This can significantly delay resolution

## Executor Removal (s.50 AJA 1985) -- Part 8 Procedure

### Specific requirements
- Issue Part 8 claim in Chancery Division (Business and Property Courts)
- Name existing executor(s) as defendant(s)
- Witness statement must address Letterstedt test criteria
- Propose replacement executor
- Draft order should include:
  - Removal of named executor
  - Appointment of replacement
  - Direction for handover of estate assets and documents
  - Costs order sought

### Common directions
- Defendant's evidence: 28 days
- Claimant's evidence in reply: 14 days
- Hearing: listed with time estimate
- Bundle: claimant to prepare per Chancery Guide

## Validation Checklist
- [ ] Correct form used (N208, not N1)
- [ ] Question for the court clearly stated
- [ ] Legal basis identified (statute/rule)
- [ ] Witness statement filed with claim form
- [ ] Draft order attached
- [ ] Court fee paid
- [ ] Service effected correctly
- [ ] Part 8 is appropriate (no substantial factual dispute)
