# Probate and Estate Administration Rules

## Executor Duties (Administration of Estates Act 1925)

### Mandatory Duties
- Arrange burial/cremation of the deceased
- Secure the deceased's property
- Collect all assets of the estate
- Pay all debts and liabilities
- Pay inheritance tax
- Distribute the estate according to the will
- Prepare estate accounts for beneficiaries

### Duty to Account
- Executors must keep accurate records of all dealings
- Beneficiaries have the right to request estate accounts at any time
- Accounts must show: assets collected, debts paid, expenses incurred, distributions made
- Failure to account can lead to court-ordered accounts (CPR Part 64)

## Key Deadlines

| Deadline | Period | Statute |
|----------|--------|---------|
| IHT payment | 6 months from end of month of death | IHT Act 1984 s.226 |
| Executor's year | 12 months from death | AEA 1925 s.44 |
| s27 notice period | 2 months from publication | Trustee Act 1925 s.27 |
| IHT corrective account | 6 months from discovering need | HMRC practice |
| Variation for IHT | 2 years from death | IHT Act 1984 s.142 |

## Grant of Probate Application

### Required documents
- Original will (and any codicils)
- Death certificate
- IHT form (IHT400 or excepted estate form)
- Executor's oath / statement of truth
- Application fee (GBP 273 for estates over GBP 5,000)

### Who can apply
- Named executor(s) in the will
- If no executor able/willing: administrator(s) under Non-Contentious Probate Rules 1987

## s27 Trustee Act Notice

### Requirements
- Publish in the London Gazette
- Publish in a newspaper circulating in the area of any land owned
- Give at least 2 months for claims
- After 2 months: executor can distribute without personal liability for unknown claims

### Wording must include
- Deceased's full name (and any former names)
- Date of death
- Address for claims
- Deadline (at least 2 months)
- Warning that estate will be distributed after deadline

## Executor Removal (s.50 AJA 1985)

### Letterstedt Test
The court's main guide is the WELFARE OF BENEFICIARIES:
- Is the estate safe in the executor's hands?
- Has trust broken down irretrievably?
- Is there a conflict of interest?
- Has the executor unreasonably delayed?
- Has the executor failed to account?

### NOT about punishment
- Removal does not require misconduct
- Court can remove executor even without fault if welfare requires it
- Minor errors are not sufficient; the estate must be at risk

## Distribution Rules

### Intestacy (no will)
Follow Administration of Estates Act 1925 s.46:
1. Spouse/civil partner: personal chattels + first GBP 322,000 + half of remainder
2. Children: the other half (or all if no spouse)
3. Parents, siblings, etc. in statutory order

### With a will
- Follow the terms of the will
- Specific legacies first, then residuary estate
- Abatement rules apply if estate is insufficient

## IHT Thresholds (2025-26)

- Nil-rate band: GBP 325,000
- Residence nil-rate band: GBP 175,000 (if qualifying property passes to direct descendants)
- Rate above threshold: 40% (36% if 10%+ to charity)
- Transferable nil-rate band: unused portion transfers to surviving spouse

## Validation Checklist
- [ ] All assets identified and valued
- [ ] IHT form completed correctly
- [ ] Grant of probate obtained before collecting assets
- [ ] s27 notice placed and 2-month period observed
- [ ] All debts paid before distribution
- [ ] Estate accounts prepared
- [ ] Distributions match will (or intestacy rules)
- [ ] Final tax returns filed
