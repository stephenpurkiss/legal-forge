# SCCO Costs Rules

## Types of Costs Assessment

### Summary Assessment (CPR 44.6)
- Done at the end of a hearing lasting less than one day
- File a costs schedule (Form N260) at least 24 hours before
- Judge assesses there and then
- No separate proceedings

### Detailed Assessment (CPR Parts 47)
- For costs after trial or where summary assessment is inappropriate
- File bill of costs within 3 months of costs order
- Points of dispute served within 21 days
- Replies served within 21 days of points
- Request hearing if not agreed

### Solicitor-Client Assessment (s.70 Solicitors Act 1974)
- Client can challenge solicitor's bill
- Must apply within 12 months of delivery (after 12 months: court permission needed)
- If bill reduced by more than 1/5: solicitor pays assessment costs
- Court can order detailed assessment of entire retainer

## Buckton Costs Categories (Estate Litigation)

### Category 1: Administration
- All costs from the estate fund
- Applications that benefit the estate as a whole

### Category 2: Beneficiary Disputes
- Usually all costs from the estate fund
- Genuine disputes arising from will ambiguity

### Category 3: Hostile Litigation
- Normal costs rules (loser pays)
- Adversarial claims between parties

## Part 36 Offers (CPR Part 36)

### Requirements for valid Part 36 offer
- State it is made under Part 36
- Specify a period of at least 21 days for acceptance
- State whether it relates to whole or part of claim
- State whether it takes into account any counterclaim

### Consequences of beating own Part 36 offer (claimant)
- Enhanced interest on damages (up to 10% above base rate)
- Indemnity costs from date of expiry
- Additional amount (up to GBP 75,000)

### Consequences of failing to beat Part 36 offer (defendant)
- Claimant pays defendant's costs from date of expiry
- On standard basis

### Part 36 in detailed assessment
- Can make Part 36 offers in costs proceedings
- Same consequences apply
- Offer relates to amount of costs to be allowed

## Payment on Account (CPR 44.2(8))

- Court MUST order payment on account unless there is good reason not to
- Reasonable proportion of likely total (usually 60-80%)
- CPR 25.6/25.7: court can order interim payment on account of costs

## Costs Budgets (CPR Part 3)

### When required
- Multi-track cases unless value exceeds GBP 25 million
- File Precedent H (costs budget)
- Costs management conference to approve budgets
- Cannot recover more than budgeted amount without good reason

### Budget format (Precedent H)
Phases: pre-action, issue, CMC, disclosure, witness statements, expert reports, PTR, trial, ADR

## Bill of Costs Format

### E-bill (mandatory for detailed assessment)
- Spreadsheet format (Precedent S)
- Phase, task, activity breakdown
- Fee earner rates
- Disbursements itemised
- VAT calculated

## Common Costs Arguments

### Proportionality (CPR 44.3(2))
Costs must be proportionate to:
- Amount in dispute
- Importance of the case
- Complexity
- Financial position of parties

### Reasonableness (CPR 44.3(1))
- Were the costs reasonably incurred?
- Are the amounts reasonable?
- Standard basis: reasonable and proportionate, doubt resolved in paying party's favour
- Indemnity basis: reasonable, doubt resolved in receiving party's favour

## Validation Checklist
- [ ] Correct costs form used (N260 for summary, bill for detailed)
- [ ] Filed within time limits
- [ ] Part 36 offer compliant (if making one)
- [ ] Buckton category correctly identified
- [ ] Fee earner rates justified
- [ ] Disbursements supported by receipts
- [ ] VAT correctly calculated
- [ ] Payment on account requested if appropriate
